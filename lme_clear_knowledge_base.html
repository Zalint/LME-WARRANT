VI. Warrants, Delivery, and Carry Costs

42 Questions and Answers covering LME Warrants, delivery procedures, weight adjustments, rent calculations, and carry cost mechanics

#1
What is the fundamental nature of an LME Warrant?
An LME Warrant is a title document (now dematerialized) that certifies possession of a specific lot of LME-approved metal stored in an approved warehouse network. Delivery of the warrant is equivalent to the delivery of the metal attached to it.
#2
What is the electronic system used by the LME for the secure transfer of LME Warrants?
The LME operates a secure electronic transfer system for LME warrants called LMEsword.
#3
How does LME Clear interface with the LMEsword system?
LME Clear maintains one or more accounts, referred to as the "LME Clear LMEsword Clearing Account," for the collection and delivery of LME Warrants.
#4
For which LME product types are LME Warrants the Underlying Asset for physical settlement?
LME Exchange Traded Forwards are Physical Delivery Contracts for which the Underlying Asset is LME Warrants.
#5
What is the standard lot size for LME Copper and High Grade Primary Aluminium warrants?
The standard lot size for LME Copper (CA) and High Grade Primary Aluminium (AH) is 25 metric tonnes.
#6
What is the maximum acceptable weight tolerance for delivered LME Warrants (Al/Cu) around the standard lot size?
The acceptable weight tolerance around 25 tonnes is ¬± 2% (approximately 24.5 t to 25.5 t).
#7
What is the risk associated with a warrant weight difference (over or under 25 t) for a trader using a paper hedge?
Weight differences create a residual delta (base risk), as the paper hedge assumes 25.0 t/lot. This modifies the economics of the trade.
#8
Which two primary financial adjustments does LME Clear apply at the time of delivery related to the physical warrant?
LME Clear applies "Weight Adjustments" (for weight difference) and "Rent and Weight Adjustments" (for accrued storage/rent).
#9
In what currency are the LME Warrant Delivery Adjustments for Rent and Weight settled?
These adjustments shall be settled in the Base Currency (USD).
#10
How is the Weight Adjustment in USD calculated?
Weight Adjustment (USD) = (Actual Weight ‚Äì 25,000 t) √ó Settlement Price (USD/t).
#11
If a Buyer receives a warrant with surplus weight (over 25,000 t), what is the financial implication regarding the Weight Adjustment?
The Buyer is debited (pays) the positive adjustment amount, as the physical metal delivered is worth more than the standard lot.
#12
If a Buyer receives a warrant with a shortage weight (under 25,000 t), what is the financial implication regarding the Weight Adjustment?
The Buyer is credited (receives funds) the negative adjustment amount, as the physical metal delivered is worth less than the standard lot.
#13
When are the Rent and Weight Adjustments typically settled via LME Clear?
They are posted to the Member's USD Settlement Account on the delivery day, for value and settlement on the next Business Day after the Prompt Date.
#14
What is the significance of LME Rent?
LME Rent refers to the accrued daily rent/storage charges billed by the LME-approved warehouse keepers.
#15
What is the basis (weight measure) used by the warehouse for invoicing LME Rent for cancelled warrants?
LME Rent is invoiced based on the standard LME lot weight for the applicable material.
#16
What must an owner do to physically remove metal from the LME system once they hold the warrant?
The owner must cancel the warrant, notifying the warehouse that the lot will exit the LME system.
#17
What must the owner settle with the warehouse after cancellation and prior to collection?
The owner must arrange full payment of LME Rent accrued and Free On Truck (FOT) charges (loading-out fees) up until the date of collection.
#18
What notice period does a warehouse (Verbrugge International) typically require to process a request for delivery out after all formalities are completed?
48 hours' notice.
#19
What action causes the LME Rent accrued under the LME Queue Based Rent Capping (QBRC) rules to be reset?
If an allocated loading slot is missed through no fault of the warehouse, rental charges remain due, and any QBRC rental charges will be reset as of the date of the missed loading slot.
#20
What is the total cost of carry formula per metric tonne (C_carry) in the context of futures P&L calculation?
C_carry is the sum of Warrant cost (C_w), LME & clearing fee (C_l), Interest (C_i), and Rent/storage (C_r).
#21
What is "Contango" and what does it reflect?
Contango is when the forward price is higher than the spot price, reflecting the costs of carrying the metal (storage, insurance, financing) and indicating a well-supplied market.
#22
What is the typical trading strategy used to profit from a market in Contango?
The classical strategy is Cash and Carry, where the trader buys metal spot (warrants) and simultaneously sells a farther-dated future to lock in the spread.
#23
What is "Backwardation" and what market condition does it signal?
Backwardation is when the spot price is higher than the prices for futures, signaling strong immediate demand or limited available stock (a premium for immediate delivery).
#24
What cost adjustment is applied against the "Contango gain" in a P&L calculation example to determine the P&L per lot?
The Cost weight adjustment is applied, which is an operational adjustment related to the difference in weight (ŒîW) between the warrant and the economic tonnage.
#25
What are the LME's shortest prompt dates?
"Cash," corresponding to a delivery two business days after the trade date (T+2), and "Tom" (Tomorrow), corresponding to delivery one business day after the trade date (T+1).
#26
What LME mechanism allows traders to defer physical delivery by one day?
The Tom-Next operation, which involves a double transaction (selling the position arriving tomorrow and buying the next expiry), allowing the position to roll by one day.
#27
What is the latest time that trading ceases for LME Warrants traded in USD, Sterling, and Euro?
Contracts must be entered into by 12:30 hours on the Business Day prior to the delivery day ("TOM Prompt Date").
#28
What is the time limit for transactions in metal/JPY currency pairs?
Trading and transaction input cease by 20:00 hours, two (2) Business Days prior to the delivery day ("cash Prompt Date").
#29
What is the deadline for LME Clear to debit Buyers for the Invoice value for LME Warrant delivery settlements in USD/EUR/GBP?
By 09:00 hours on the Prompt Date.
#30
What is the estimated time when LME Clear credits Sellers for the Settlement Payment (Account Sale value) in USD/EUR/GBP?
Approximately 12:30 hours on the Prompt Date, following the receipt of LME Warrants from Sellers.
#31
When must Sellers provide LME Warrants to fulfill their Delivery Obligation?
Sellers shall nominate all necessary warrants in an "Authorised" Warrant Transfer Instruction by 11:00 hours on the Prompt Date.
#32
What is the LME Premium Warrant Payment Obligation?
It is the obligation of a Buyer under an LME Aluminium Premium Contract to deliver LME Warrants, together with cash, in part satisfaction of the Payment Obligation.
#33
What must be true about the rent status of LME Warrants for them to be accepted as Eligible Collateral?
There must be no outstanding rent payments and no Rent Date must occur within five (5) Business Days of the eligibility assessment.
#34
What reports does LMEsword make available regarding delivery adjustments?
The Warrant Collection Adjustment Invoice (for seller) and the Warrant Allocation Adjustment Invoice (for buyer).
#35
How does LME Clear determine the net Delivery Position for all open physical contracts at expiry on a given Account?
It calculates a Final Delivery Position by netting the Purchase Positions and Sale Positions across all currencies for that Underlying Asset on the Account.
#36
How many Warrant Movements may a Member have with LME Clear in a single Underlying Asset (excluding LME Premium Warrants) on a single Business Day?
A Member may have a maximum of three (3) Warrant Movements with LME Clear: one net movement for House Accounts, one total buying movement for Client Accounts, and one total selling movement for Client Accounts.
#37
When LME Clear applies a Buy-In due to a Member's delivery failure, what costs must the Member reimburse LME Clear for?
The Member must reimburse LME Clear for all costs incurred in purchasing or borrowing the Underlying Asset, including the amount paid and execution costs.
#38
If an auction is declared a "Failed Auction," what steps may LME Clear take?
LME Clear may undertake one or more further auctions and may adjust the parameters of the new auction.
#39
What is the conversion fee for re-warranting (issuing a new warrant) if the load out slots were already scheduled?
The charge is USD 8 per ton for metals like Aluminium Alloy and Copper.
#40
What is the definition of a "Rent Date" for an LME Warrant?
The day on which rent falls due, which shall be 31 March in each calendar year (unless otherwise specified).
#41
How is the Settlement Payment value calculated for Invoice and Account Sales?
Amount = Contract weight √ó Settlement Price √ó lots.
#42
Which section of the Clearing Procedures defines the LME Warrant Delivery Procedures?
Clearing Procedure Part F, Section 2.

VIII. Contextualisation (Conversation Bank ABC/Macquarie)

42 Questions and Answers covering the specific relationship and operational procedures between Bank ABC and Macquarie in LME warrant transactions

#43
What is Macquarie's primary role in relation to Bank ABC's LME activity?
Macquarie acts as the clearing member or broker for Bank ABC, as Bank ABC is not a direct member of the LME.
#44
How does Bank ABC, as a client, generally acquire or dispose of warrant positions?
This activity happens through futures or forwards look-alike transactions executed on execution platforms and cleared via Macquarie's membership.
#45
Can a client like Bank ABC directly submit a sell order for warrants through the broker's system?
No, warrants are primarily used to satisfy a delivery obligation against the expiry of a forward or future position.
#46
When a long position expires resulting in a physical delivery, does the trader know the specific warrants they will receive upfront?
No, the trader receives an allocation of warrants directly into LMEsword, and they cannot control which warrants are received.
#47
When a short position results in a delivery obligation, can the trader specify which warrants they return to the exchange?
Yes, when delivering them back, the trader can specify which warrants they are sending back (referred to as the "sifting piece").
#48
How does the client typically communicate to Macquarie which specific warrants to deliver back to the exchange on a short position?
The client confirms to Macquarie which warrants are being sent back, often using the same CSV file format provided by Macquarie, identifying the warrants by ID.
#49
What is the expected deadline for the client to confirm which warrants they will deliver for a position prompting the next day?
Confirmation is generally expected by the close of business on the current day.
#50
What specific data must the inventory file (CSV) contain to enable the delivery instruction?
It must contain the individual warrant ID and other salient information for each warrant.
#51
Why does the trader initially deem having the full inventory of warrant IDs "useless" for the system solution?
Because the trader hedges that position on the market using the common base metal price (forward price), and details like custodian or location are not required for general hedging.
#52
Does Macquarie provide market data reflecting physical spreads based on warrant location or characteristics for mark-to-market?
No, Macquarie does not provide valuation or spreading information based on warrant characteristics or location.
#53
What is the consequence if the rent fees are not accounted for "along the way" by the client?
The trader's Profit & Loss (P&L) will not accurately capture these fees until settlement.
#54
Why can Bank ABC not compute the rent fees internally using their system (M)?
Because Bank ABC is not a direct LME member, they lack access to LMEsword, where the complex rent management and fee computation (based on warrant ID, location, etc.) are handled.
#55
Where do the Rent and Weight Adjustment fees originate?
This cost is a pass-through from the exchange.
#56
When are Macquarie's own brokerage commission fees settled relative to the warrant activity?
The commission piece is attached to the warrant activity and processed in line with the delivery schedule, not through a monthly invoicing process.
#57
What is Macquarie's commission rate for warrant activity?
The agreed rate is 140 per lot.
#58
What daily report does Macquarie typically provide to Bank ABC regarding their warrant holdings?
A list of the inventory is provided on a daily basis via CSV files.
#59
For risk management, what type of view does the trader need for positions up until three months?
A cumulative view (stock mode) up until three months, using the three months as a base.
#60
For risk management, what type of view does the trader need for positions after three months?
A reverse cumulative view (reverse stock mode).
#61
What type of risk management view is required for fixed-price contracts like swaps?
A Delta view (or fixing view) bucketed by the underlying future pillar.
#62
What data format constraint highlights the need for automation in the client's system?
The list of warrants can be 5,000 lines, requiring an automated function ("plug") rather than line-by-line manual entry.
#63
How is physical delivery generally managed in the client's system if the delivery event is used instead of the future/option exercise event?
The warrants are inserted manually (via file received from the clearer) to close the short against the delivery.
#64
What specific LME events are commonly packaged into system modules (like M) to handle warrants?
Specific LME events such as take off, break on, and give up are packaged to manage the warrants.
#65
If LME Clear requires a Member to allocate Rent and Weight Debits/Credits to a Client Account, what is the deadline for notification?
The Member must notify LME Clear prior to the Close of Business on the day the relevant LME Warrant was delivered.
#66
What level of position detail does LME Clear track for Gross Omnibus Segregated Client Accounts regarding warrant movements?
LME Clear determines warrant movements on an Account-level basis; the Member is solely responsible for managing the allocation of warrants to individual Allocation IDs.
#67
What is the client's general obligation regarding compliance with the LME Rules and third parties?
The Member must ensure that the Client is aware and understands that LME Clear shall not have any obligations or liability to any person who is not a Member.
#68
Can LME Clear disclose Auction Portfolio information to a client assisting a Member's bid?
Yes, if the Member obtained LME Clear's consent and the specific Client signed a confidentiality agreement prior to the commencement of the Default Period.
#69
Which electronic terminal is noted for its role in the LME warrant delivery process by the broker?
LMEsword is identified as the exchange's delivery terminal where warrant information is held.
#70
What type of transfer instruction is used in LMEsword to move Eligible LME Warrants to or from the LME Clear Pledged Account?
Transfers are executed via an Ex-cleared Transfer Instruction.
#71
If the broker needs to clarify a bid submitted in an auction, whom may LME Clear contact?
LME Clear may contact an Authorised Representative of the Member or an alternative authorised individual whose contact details the Member specified in advance.
#72
Who is responsible for ensuring that all transferring Clients consent to an Account Transfer?
It is the responsibility of the Requesting Member(s) to ensure full consent of all transferring Clients/Indirect Clients.
#73
What is the nature of the LME Group's involvement in the physical storage of metal?
The LME relies on a worldwide network of approved depots (warehouses) that store the metal, but the LME owns none of the warehouses itself.
#74
What LME feature gives the Seller the right to choose which specific warrant they deliver against a contract?
Delivery is made at the Seller's option, meaning the Seller can select the warehouse and brand, provided they are LME approved (delivery via warrant).
#75
In a delivery scenario, why is metal stock published by the LME followed closely by traders?
The LME publishes stock "on warrant" versus "cancelled warrants" daily to inform the market of physical availability and help traders anticipate delivery tensions.
#76
If a load out slot is missed due to the Member's fault, what outstanding charges remain due?
All outstanding rental charges will be due and payable up until the rescheduled loading slot date.
#77
What are LME Premium Warrants defined as?
LME Warrants that are allocable to a specific geographical region.
#78
What is the maximum number of days trading in the expiring LME Aluminium Premium Contract is permitted prior to the Prompt Date?
Trading ceases two (2) Business Days before the Prompt Date (the third Wednesday of the month).
#79
How is the transfer of LME Warrant Collateral managed in the Client Direct Posting Structure?
The Posting Client instructs its Member to make an Ex-cleared Transfer Instruction in LMEsword to the LME Clear Pledged Account.
#80
What is the main characteristic of the "Unauthorised" Warrant Transfer Instruction received by a Seller on the day prior to Prompt Date?
The Seller receives an instruction which they may discard and create a new instruction, confirming the ability of the Seller to choose the warrant.
#81
What happens if LME Clear cannot deliver a cash payment in Renminbi (CNH) due to liquidity or currency controls?
LME Clear may postpone the return of Cash Collateral denominated in renminbi.
#82
If a Mandatory Bidder fails to participate in an auction, what percentage of their Default Fund Contribution (DFC) is applied toward the resulting Excess Loss?
LME Clear applies 100% of the DFC of any Mandatory Bidder who failed to submit a bid.
#83
Can a non-defaulting Member request an exclusion from mandatory bidding?
Yes, LME Clear may exclude a Mandatory Bidder if LME Clear determines that such exclusion is desirable.
#84
Where can LME Clear's full list of available reports be found?
In Annex 4 (List of Available Reports), the content of which is set out on the LME Clear Website.

What is the function of a House Account?
A House Account may only be used to record the rights and obligations arising in respect of Contracts resulting from Transactions effected on the Member's own behalf.

#86
What mechanism in a Direct Gross Omnibus Segregated Client Account allows LME Clear to calculate separate Margin Requirements?
Positions are segregated and identifiable by Allocation IDs.

What consequence does the inclusion of a Non-Identified Client have on a Client Account's portability?
The inclusion of a Non-Identified Client will impair the ability of all Clients allocated to that Account to apply the Porting Process upon the Member's default.

#88
What restriction applies to the destination of assets and positions transferred out of a Client Account during the Porting Process?
Transfer is only permitted to a single Member Transferee that consents to receive such assets and positions.
Section III. Accounts, Client Business, and Portability
#89
Must collateral provided to cover delivery risk be in cash or non-cash form?
Any amounts required to cover delivery risk will be payable in cash.
Section IV. Collateral, Margin, and Treasury
#90
What legal status does Cash Collateral provided by a Member hold with LME Clear?
Cash Collateral is freely usable by LME Clear; the Member retains no proprietary interest in the cash, which constitutes an unsecured debt owed by LME Clear to the Member.
Section IV. Collateral, Margin, and Treasury
#91
How is the "Collateral Value" of Eligible Collateral determined?
The Collateral Value is the Market Value of that Eligible Collateral (in the Base Currency) minus any applicable Haircut.
Section IV. Collateral, Margin, and Treasury
#92
What is the general priority of collateral usage in the Cover Distribution Process?
Available Non-cash Collateral shall be used before Available Cash Collateral, regardless of Haircut or currency, except up to the amount of the Minimum Cash Requirement.
Section IV. Collateral, Margin, and Treasury
#93
What is the primary objective LME Clear must protect when taking default action?
LME Clear's primary objective is "effective risk management," which means protecting (i) its capital and the Default Fund, and (ii) the integrity of the markets which it clears.
Section V. Default and Risk Management
#94
What is the first resource LME Clear applies to discharge a "Default Loss"?
The Collateral, or any Asset Cover, provided by the Defaulting Member.
Section V. Default and Risk Management
#95
What restriction applies to the composition of an Auction Portfolio for loss allocation?
An individual Portfolio created for auction shall not contain Open Contracts relating to both the LME Base Service and the LMEprecious Service.
Section V. Default and Risk Management
#96
What are LME Clear's options if it is impossible or impracticable to Buy-In an Underlying Asset after a Member's delivery failure?
LME Clear may make a Cash Settlement (Invoice Back) to the non-defaulting Member in substitution for the physical delivery.
Section V. Default and Risk Management
#97
What are the two types of compression structure a Member can elect for an Account?
Manual or File Based Compression or Automatic Compression.
Section VII. Acceptance and Compression
#98
For a contract to be eligible for Compression, what key requirement must be met if the transaction resulted in Underlying Client Trades?
The same Client or Indirect Client must be a party to each such Underlying Client Trade.
Section VII. Acceptance and Compression
#99
Name the four components that constitute the "Core Economic Terms" of a Contract for compression eligibility.
The Prompt Date, the Eligible Product, the type of Underlying Asset (not quantity), and the type of Settlement Currency (not quantity).
Section VII. Acceptance and Compression
#100
What restriction does compression impose on the calculation of the contingent margin (DCVM)?
The compression must ensure that the DCVM attributable to the resulting Post-Compression Contracts remains the same as the sum of the DCVM for the original Contracts.
